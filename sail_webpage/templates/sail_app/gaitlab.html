Wearable Gait Lab - Risk Factors Identification for Work-related Injuries

(w/ Dr. Wenyao Xu at Unversity at Buffalo)

Foot is a complex and critical part of the human body to realize locomotion. Sensing from foot, importance information about human gait, postures of activities, and walking trajectory could be obtained, which could contribute to applications such as healthcare, worker safety, and indoor localization. In this research, an innovative wearable system—Wearable Gait Lab (WGL) was designed to monitor plantar pressure and foot motion.
Design advantages

  1. Hidden design. All the hardware devices were nested into an insole shaped case to make WGL an easily acceptable device that could be used the same as a normal insole.
  2. Customized design. A customized design method was proposed and applied on WGL, which makes it possible for WGL to be trimmed to fit different foot sizes with one design. The customized design also contributes to decrease the manufacturing cost and make WGL affordable to most people.
Applications exploration

  1. Worker safety. WGL was applied to discriminate risk factors of work-related musculoskeletal disorders such as overexertion, awkward postures and excessive repetition.
  2. Indoor localization. A Known Velocity Update and Double-Foot Position Calibration (KUPT + DFPC) method was proposed and applied with WGL to help reduce the accumulated drift of accelerometer. KUPT could measure the velocity from heel-strike to toe-off based on the recorded real-time foot angle and the shoe dimensions, which increases the time period when the velocity could be measured from 24% to 62% of a whole gait cycle time when compared with Zero Velocity Update (ZUPT). DFPC method could fuse the position information of both feet based on the symmetrical characteristic of human gait to further increase the reliability of the localization results.
